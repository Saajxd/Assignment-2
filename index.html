Introduction

The World Happiness Report is a landmark survey of the state of global happiness that ranks 156 countries by how happy their citizens perceive themselves to be. The World Happiness Report 2019 focuses on happiness and the community, with a focus on the technologies, social norms, conflicts and government policies that have driven those changes. The happiness scores and rankings use data from the Gallup World Poll. The scores are based on answers to the main life evaluation question asked in the poll. This question, known as the Cantril ladder, asks respondents to think of a ladder with the best possible life for them being a 10 and the worst possible life being a 0 and to rate their own current lives on that scale. The scores are from nationally representative samples for the year 2019 and use the Gallup weights to make the estimates representative.

Economy Vs Happiness Score

The above scatterplot depicts the correlation between Economy and Happiness Score. It can be observed that there is a positive correlation between the two factors. This means that as a countries GDP increases, so does the Happiness Score. The main reason for this would be as GDP increases, so does the overall ability of the countries ability to develop in terms of infrastructure, security and other systems. This in turn leads to higher employment rates and increase in quality of life leading to happier citizens.
